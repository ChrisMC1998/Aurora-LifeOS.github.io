    What Life OS means

    Every other page in Aurora is about input — capturing ideas, sorting them, tracking your mood and energy. Life OS is the page that asks the question those other pages don't: what actually happened to that idea afterward?

    Most "save for later" apps are where good ideas go to die — Inbox grows, nothing changes. Aurora's whole premise is that a saved idea should become a system, not a bookmark: a goal you set, a habit you repeat, a reminder that nudges you, and eventually a number on a dashboard that proves it worked. Life OS visualizes that pipeline so you can see — at a glance — whether your captures are turning into change, or just piling up.

    The chain below traces one real idea from your Library through that pipeline. As you move ideas through Inbox → Scheduled → Reviewing → Testing → Applied → Completed on the Library and Experiments pages, this view updates to reflect your furthest-progressed idea — proof that the system is doing what it claims.
